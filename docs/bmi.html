What is BMI (Body Mass Index)?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation using your height and weight. It helps determine whether your weight is healthy for your height. The result gives you an idea if you're underweight, normal, overweight, or obese. BMI is not a perfect tool but works well for general health screening. It's widely used by doctors, fitness trainers, and health professionals. Understanding your BMI can be a great first step toward better health.



BMI Categories

BMI falls into different ranges that tell you about your body weight status. These categories help you understand if you're at a healthy weight or need changes.


  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal (Healthy): BMI between 18.5 and 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9
  • Obese: BMI 30 or more

Each category can affect your health differently, so it's good to know where you stand.



BMI for Adults

BMI is most accurate for adults aged 18 to 65 years. It does not consider muscle mass, bone structure, or fat distribution. For example, athletes may have a high BMI due to muscle, not fat. It also may not be very accurate for elderly people or those with specific health conditions. For children and teenagers, doctors use a different growth chart, not BMI alone. Always talk to a healthcare provider for a full understanding of your health.



Benefits of Knowing Your BMI

Helps you know if your weight is in a healthy range or not. Gives you a starting point for making better health decisions. Motivates you to exercise, eat better, or maintain your lifestyle. Helps prevent diseases related to being overweight or underweight. Makes you more aware of your body and personal wellness.
